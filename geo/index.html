Make sure AI tools recommend you, not your competitors

Search engines are no longer the only gatekeepers. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude are answering questions your website should be answering. If they don't know you exist, you're losing business.

The problem

AI tools don't browse the web like humans do. They look for entities: clearly defined businesses with specific expertise, verifiable credentials, and consistent signals across the web. They evaluate whether a business is credible enough to recommend.

Most business websites weren't built for this. The content is there, but the signals are missing. AI systems can't determine what your business actually is, who it serves, or why it should be cited over a competitor. The result is invisibility in the fastest-growing discovery channel.

How we approach it

Entity resolution

We define your business as a clear, distinct entity: what it does, who it serves, where it operates, and what credentials back it up. AI systems need this to cite you accurately.

Structured data

Schema markup that tells AI systems exactly what your business is. Organisation, Person, Service, FAQ, deployed so machines can parse it without guessing.

Authority signals

Key person development, consistent cross-web presence, and credibility markers that AI systems evaluate when deciding whether to recommend you.

AI visibility baseline

We measure how you currently appear across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude. This gives you a concrete "before" picture to track progress against.

What changes

AI tools start recommending your business when people ask for help in your sector. Your entity signals are clear and consistent. You show up in AI-generated answers, comparisons, and recommendations, alongside or ahead of competitors who haven't done this work yet.
