# CONTACT / CONVERSATION STARTER WIREFRAME

**Page Name:** Contact / Conversation Starter
**URL:** /contact/
**Tier:** Tier 2 — Trust & Navigation (Bridge Page)
**Page Type:** Conversion Gate
**Silo:** Final conversion point from all silos
**Primary Persona:** All personas (final commitment stage across all entry paths)

---

## PAGE-LEVEL METADATA

**H1:** "Let's have a conversation. No sales call."

**Meta Description Angle:** Start a conversation with WebXC principals. We'll listen, ask questions, and be direct about fit. Book time to chat.

**Content Angle:** This page exists as the final conversion point and friction reducer. It removes barriers by being honest about what happens next. The page should feel welcoming and direct, emphasizing that the first conversation is exploratory, not transactional. All prior pages (Homepage, About, Services, Blog, Discovery) route here — the Contact page is the single, unified conversion gate.

**Agitation Approach:** Minimal (Conversion Gate)
**Agitation Depth:** MINIMAL (0-40 words)
*Rationale: Visitor has already decided to engage. No need to surface problems — they're motivated. Focus entirely on removing final objections to action.*

**Page Focus:** Conversion completion + transparency + reassurance
**Emotional Arc:** Curiosity (hero) → Confidence (what to expect) → Commitment (form) → Clarity (next steps)

**[SCROLL POSITION: Final Conversion]**

[FORM TITLE / HEADING]

"Tell us about your business and what you're looking to explore. We'll respond

[TOPICAL PURPOSE: This is the core conversion element. The form captures just enough

[CONTEXT DEPTH: DEEP]

```

#### [FORM FIELDS — STRUCTURE]

```
[FIELD GROUP 1: Identity]

[FIELD: Full Name]
Required: Yes
Placeholder: "Your name"
Tone: Conversational
*Rationale: Basic identity. Signals respect and personalization.*

[FIELD: Business Type / Industry]
Type: Dropdown or short text
Required: Yes
Options: (e.g., "Professional Services", "B2B SaaS", "E-commerce", "Healthcare",
"Manufacturing", "Other — tell us")
Placeholder: "E.g., Digital Marketing Agency, Medical Practice, Engineering Firm"
Tone: Specific enough to enable routing, not exhaustive
*Rationale: Helps principal understand business context. Signals whether industry
expertise (e.g., medical YMYL, professional standards) applies.*

[FIELD: Email Address]
Type: Email input
Required: Yes
Placeholder: "your@email.com"
*Rationale: Primary contact method.*

[FIELD: Phone Number]
Type: Tel input
Required: No
Placeholder: "+64 (your number)"
*Rationale: Optional but captured. Enables choice of contact method.*

---

[FIELD GROUP 2: Context & Situation]

[FIELD: Best Way to Reach You?]
Type: Radio buttons or dropdown
Options:
  - "Email"
  - "Phone"
  - "Book a time in my calendar"
Required: Yes
*Rationale: Respects visitor preference and reduces friction. If "Book a time"
selected, form can conditionally display calendar link.*

[FIELD: Tell Us About Your Situation]
Type: Textarea (expandable)
Required: Yes
Character limit: 500–1000 characters suggested
Placeholder: "What's prompting the conversation? What's your main challenge or
question about your digital presence?"
Tone: Open-ended, inviting narrative, not leading
*Rationale: Most important field. Captures context for the principal to review
before the call. Enables pre-call prep and fit assessment. The principal reads
this and prepares a thoughtful, targeted response.*

---

[FIELD GROUP 3: Optional Context (Secondary)]

[FIELD: How Did You Hear About Us?]
Type: Dropdown or select
Required: No
Options:
  - "Google Search"
  - "Blog Post"
  - "Social Media"
  - "Referral / Word of Mouth"
  - "LinkedIn"
  - "Other"
*Rationale: Attribution and channel tracking. Helps WebXC understand which
entry points drive conversions.*

[FIELD: Do You Already Have Some of This Work Done?]
Type: Radio buttons or toggle
Options:
  - "No — we're starting from scratch"
  - "Yes — we have positioning/strategy already"
  - "Partially — we have some work but need alignment"
  - "I'm not sure"
Required: No
*Rationale: Signals whether Discovery should focus on audit of existing work
or greenfield strategy. Helps principal tailor first conversation.*

[FORM DESIGN NOTES]

[VISUAL HIERARCHY: Primary — entire form section]
Form should be visually clean, one column, uncluttered. No aggressive scarcity
language ("Limited spots available", "Act now"). Trust-building language instead.

[MOBILE OPTIMIZATION]
Form fields stack vertically. Touch-friendly input sizing (44px minimum tap targets).
No inline validation errors (post-submission only). Consider single-column layout
on mobile, 2-column on desktop if form grows.

[ACCESSIBILITY]
All fields have clear labels, not just placeholders. Error messages are specific
and supportive ("Please enter a valid email" not "Error in field 2").
ARIA labels where needed. Form is keyboard navigable.
