**Page Name:** Blog Post Detail Page (Template)

**Silo:** Content & Thought Leadership
**Primary Persona:** Strategically Unaware Leaders (entry), all personas (content value)

---

## Wireframe Metadata

**Content Strategy:**
Individual blog posts establish WebXC's authority and provide free value before any conversation. Written by principals (James or Andy). Each post targets specific long-tail queries and links contextually to service pages. Post structure: Hero (title, date, author, read time), Body (800-2000 words, H2/H3 sections), Mid-post CTA, End-of-post CTA, Related Posts, Author Bio.

**Agitation Approach:** Implied / Positive contrast
**Agitation Depth:** LIGHT (varies by post topic — problems appear organically within educational content, not as dedicated agitation sections)

**SEO Intent:**
Each blog post targets a specific long-tail query or question related to WebXC's authority domains (digital authority, positioning, transformation, team capability, market advantage). The post structures semantic coverage of the topic, establishes expertise, and contextually guides readers to relevant service pages.

**H1 Strategic Intent:**
The H1 is the post title. It must contain the primary search intent keyword/phrase and be specific enough to define the post's core topic. The H1 should position WebXC's perspective as distinct from generic industry content.

**[SCHEMA: BreadcrumbList]** – Enables search engines to understand page hierarchy: Home > Blog > [Post Category] > [Post Title]
**[SCHEMA: BlogPosting]** – Primary schema for this page type. Includes author, datePublished, dateModified, headline, description, wordCount, articleBody.
**[SCHEMA: FAQPage]** – If post contains a FAQ section.

### SECTION 2: [OPENING / CONTEXT-SETTING PARAGRAPH]

**Scroll Position:** 10–15%
**Visual Priority:** Secondary
**Context Depth:** BROAD
**Word Count Target:** 150–200 words

**Structure:**

```
[INTRODUCTORY PARAGRAPH]
2–3 sentences that:
1. Hook the reader with a relatable tension or question.
2. Establish why this topic matters to their business right now.
3. Preview the post's value (what they'll learn or understand by the end).

Example framing:
"[Business situation/challenge] is [problem manifestation].
Most [target audience] respond by [common ineffective approach].
But what actually works is [WebXC's perspective/framework]."
```

**Topical Purpose:**
This section lowers friction by making the post feel personally relevant. It creates a context contract: "If you read this, you'll understand X." It also establishes the agitation depth for this post (light, since we're in educational mode, not sales mode).

**Semantic SEO Annotations:**

- **[TOPICAL PURPOSE: Context-setting & reader positioning]** "Establishes that this post addresses a real business problem and positions the reader as someone who stands to benefit. Agent: WebXC author, Action: contextualizes the problem, Beneficiary: business owner with implicit pain point related to [topic]."
- **[AGITATION APPROACH: Positive contrast]** "Problem appears implicitly as a contrast with the author's recommendation, not as standalone fear language."
- **[FRAME ELEMENT: Reader as protagonist]** "The reader is positioned as someone actively grappling with this challenge and seeking clarity."

### SECTION 4: [MID-POST CTA - CONTEXTUAL]

**Scroll Position:** 60–70% (after main content, before conclusion/related posts)
**Visual Priority:** Primary (stands out visually, but not intrusive)
**Context Depth:** BROAD

**Structure:**

```
[CTA BLOCK CONTAINER - LIGHT BACKGROUND COLOR]

[SUPPORTING TEXT]
1–2 sentences contextual to the post topic.
"If you're ready to [post's implied next step], here's what to do next."

[CTA BUTTON]
Topic-specific CTA. Options:
- "Ready to assess your [topic domain]?" → /workshop/
- "Want a strategic review?" → /discovery/
- "Learn how we approach [topic]." → /services/[specific-service]/

[SUPPORTING LINK - OPTIONAL]
Secondary link (not a button) for less-decided readers.
"Or explore related content: [link to another blog post]"
```

**Topical Purpose:**
At the 60% mark, the reader has received substantial value. This CTA offers a natural next step for readers who are now curious about WebXC's approach or ready to explore paid engagement (workshop, discovery, or service page). The CTA is topic-specific, not generic.

**Semantic SEO Annotations:**

- **[TOPICAL PURPOSE: Value-to-action transition]** "Offers the reader a clear next step after consuming free educational content. Agent: WebXC, Action: invites reader to self-assess or book consultation, Beneficiary: reader ready to move from learning to engagement."
- **[INTERNAL LINK: CTA Button]** "Routes to discovery (problem diagnosis), workshop (self-assessment), or specific service page. Purpose: low-friction entry into paid engagement."
- **[INTERNAL LINK: Secondary Link]** "Provides alternative path for less-decided readers, increasing retention on content silo."
- **[CTA PLACEMENT: Mid-post value transition]** "Placed after substantial value delivered, but before post conclusion. Respects reader agency — no pressure, but clear offer."

## Internal Linking Architecture

**Hub-to-Detail Linking:**
- Blog Hub page (/blog/) links to all published blog posts
- Each blog post links back to Blog Hub

**Topical Clustering:**
- Posts within the same semantic domain (e.g., "Authority & Positioning") link to each other via Related Posts section
- Ensures semantic authority passing and topical clustering signals to search engines

**Silo Bridges:**
- Blog posts link to discovery page (/discovery/) when post content implies diagnostic value (mid-post CTA)
- Blog posts link to relevant service pages (/services/[option]/) when post content naturally connects to a solution
- Blog posts link to workshop (/workshop/) for posts targeting early-stage research personas
- Blog posts link to About page (/about/) via Author Bio section

**Cross-Silo Linking:**
- Mid-post CTA: `[INTERNAL LINK: To /discovery/ — Purpose: "Convert learning into professional diagnosis"]`
- Mid-post CTA: `[INTERNAL LINK: To /workshop/ — Purpose: "Invite self-assessment for less-decided readers"]`
- Mid-post CTA: `[INTERNAL LINK: To /services/[specific-service]/ — Purpose: "Route content interest into service exploration"]`
- Related Posts: `[INTERNAL LINKS: To 3 related blog posts — Purpose: "Topical depth and authority clustering"]`
- Author Bio: `[INTERNAL LINK: To /about/ — Purpose: "Principal credibility and brand awareness"]`

**Linking Frequency:**
- Mid-post CTA: 1 primary link (1 destination only; no competing CTAs)
- Related Posts: 3 topical links
- Author Bio: 1 trust link to About page
- **Total internal links per post: 5 (1 mid-post CTA + 3 related + 1 author bio)**

Avoid excessive linking (no more than 1 CTA link per 200 words of body content).

## Post Publication Checklist

Before publishing a blog post using this template:

- [ ] **H1 is specific and keyword-rich.** Does it contain the primary search intent?
- [ ] **Post structure is semantic.** Headings form a coherent outline when read alone.
- [ ] **All core predicates are covered.** Every foundational question about the topic is addressed.
- [ ] **Mid-post CTA is contextual.** The CTA button makes sense at the 60% scroll mark.
- [ ] **Related Posts are truly related.** The 3 linked posts share topical overlap and progression logic.
- [ ] **Author Bio links to /about/.** Entire block is clickable and encourages principal credibility building.
- [ ] **All internal links use correct URLs.** No broken links or typos in /blog/, /discovery/, /services/, /about/, /workshop/.

- [ ] **Post passes Readability check.** Sentences are concise, vocabulary is accessible, subheadings break up content.
- [ ] **Allocation Summary is completed.** Tracked in global content calendar or allocation registry.

## Notes for Copy Skill

When writing copy for a blog post using this template:

1. **H1 & Opening:** The opening 2–3 sentences must hook the reader with a tension or question that makes the post feel personally relevant to their business situation.

2. **H2 Sections:** Each H2 should be defensible as a distinct concept. Within the post, H2s should progress from foundational (what/why) to applied (how/when).

3. **Tone:** Write as a peer educator, not a salesperson. Use "we" and "you" naturally. Avoid jargon unless you explain it. Assume the reader is intelligent but unfamiliar with WebXC's specific framework.

4. **Agitation (Light):** Problems should emerge organically within educational content. Don't write dedicated "pain point" paragraphs. Instead, contrast the common approach with the better approach. Example: "Most businesses treat [topic] as a cost center. But when you reframe it as [different perspective], the ROI becomes obvious."

5. **Mid-Post CTA Placement:** After the final H2 section, before the Conclusion, place the contextual CTA. Make the surrounding 1–2 sentences explain why now is the natural moment for the reader to engage further.

6. **Related Posts:** Choose 3 posts that expand on this post's themes. If this post is "Why" and "What," related posts might be "How," case examples, or adjacent challenges.

7. **Author Bio:** Keep it to 50–100 words. Focus on relevant domain experience and why the author has earned the right to write on this topic. Avoid generic "writer with passion for X" language.

8. **Interactive Elements:** If using an expandable FAQ, each Q&A pair should target a long-tail query or "People Also Ask" variation. If using a checklist, ensure it's actionable and maps to the post's core framework.
