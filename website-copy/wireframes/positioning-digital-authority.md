[AGITATION CONTENT — Narrative structure, not bullets]

**Opening statement:** The gap between what your business is and what your digital presence says about it is costing you visibility, customers, and competitive advantage.

**Three specific fears to layer:**

1. **Positioning Ambiguity:** Your positioning is generic or unclear. Competitors with weaker products but clearer positioning are getting noticed. Your website says what you do but not why you're genuinely different or why buyers should choose you. Search engines and AI systems see you as interchangeable.

2. **Content for Volume, Not Authority:** You've published content to "stay visible," but it's thin, repetitive, or misaligned with how your buyers actually research solutions. AI systems can't extract credible evidence of your expertise from your site. ChatGPT and Perplexity don't cite you. Your thought leadership exists in your head, not in your digital presence.

3. **Entity Invisibility:** Your business and the people who founded it aren't clearly registered as credible entities in AI and search systems. AI can't verify who you are, what you've built, or why it matters. You're discoverable only if someone already knows your company name — not when they're researching the category.

**Closing tension line:** Meanwhile, your competitors with clearer positioning are appearing in AI-assisted discovery channels. The window to establish authority is open — and closing.
