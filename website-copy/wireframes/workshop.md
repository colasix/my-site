## NOTES FOR COPYWRITER

- **Assessment form tone:** Conversational, no jargon. Focus on outcomes ("What's your biggest challenge?") not process ("What's your current SEO strategy?").
- **Results preview mockup:** Should feel professional but accessible. Avoid clinical/corporate audit language. Use color/visual hierarchy to distinguish strengths vs. gaps without judgment.
- **Results email copy:** Must include clear next-step options + reassurance ("Take your time deciding"). Include relevant blog links based on their top gaps.
- **FAQ answers:** Keep short (2-3 sentences) for scannable format. Link to deeper content on other pages where appropriate (e.g., "Learn more about Discovery here").
- **Section 3 agitation:** Balance honest problem recognition with confidence in solutions. Do NOT use fear/urgency language. Example acceptable: "Most businesses can't see the connection between their digital presence and results." Example to avoid: "Your business is probably invisible online and bleeding customers."
- **Section 7 next-step copy:** Each pathway should feel equally valid and judgment-free. No subtle pressure toward Discovery or services. Emphasize: business owner chooses based on their situation.

---

## NOTES FOR DESIGNER

- **Hero visual:** Should convey professionalism and clarity. Avoid generic "person at desk" stock imagery. Consider: business leader reviewing strategic framework, or abstract audit/clarity visual. Color palette: maintain WebXC confidence brand (not playful, not corporate sterile).
- **Form design:** Multi-step on mobile (progress bar visible). Input fields: large touch targets, clear labels. Helper text appears inline or on hover (not required for all fields). Form background: subtle contrast from page background (not alarming, clearly distinct section).
- **Results preview mockup:** Use actual interface language and formatting. Mock data should be realistic (not overly positive, not alarming). Color scheme: green/positive for strengths, yellow/attention for gaps, blue for recommendations. Typography: hierarchy clear between scores, findings, and recommendations.
- **FAQ accordion:** Expanded answers should not dominate visual real estate. Subtle animation on expand/collapse. Maintains scannable format (not walls of text).
- **CTA buttons:** Primary CTAs (blue/brand color) in hero and closing. Secondary CTAs in lighter weight where offering alternatives (e.g., "Or Schedule a Conversation"). Always match CTA styling to site-wide button system.
- **Mobile stacking:** Hero text + image → vertical stack. Form fields → full width. CTAs → full-width buttons (not small click targets).
