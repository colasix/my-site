# Contact Page Wireframe: WebXC (Focused)

This is the conversion endpoint for all WebXC CTAs. Purpose: Move from exploration to conversation. Remove friction through honesty. Keep tone peer-to-peer, direct, low-pressure.

**H1:** "Let's have a conversation. No sales call."

---

## Section 1: Hero + Assurance

**H2:** "James and Andy handle every conversation personally. We listen first, pitch second."

**Purpose:** Signal that this is genuinely low-pressure. Establish that principals respond, not a sales team. Set tone of respect and peer-to-peer engagement.

---

## Section 2: What to Expect

**H2:** "Here's what happens next"

Three declarative statements (not bullets):

1. We'll respond within 24 hours and suggest a time that works for you.
2. We'll ask about your business, what's not working, and whether WebXC is a fit.
3. No pitch. No high-pressure follow-up. You'll know where we stand after the call.

**Purpose:** Set expectations. Remove fear of sales funnel. Honesty itself is the conversion lever.

---

## Section 3: The Form

**H2:** "Tell us about your situation"

**Form fields (in order):**

1. Name (required) — "Just the name we should call you"
2. Business type / industry (required) — "What sector? E.g., software, construction, professional services"
3. Email (required) — "Where we'll reach you"
4. Phone (optional) — "Mobile preferred"
5. Best way to reach you (required, radio: Phone / Email / Calendar link)
6. Tell us about your situation (required, text area) — Placeholder: "What brought you here? What's the core issue? Timeline?"
7. How did you hear about us (optional, dropdown: Blog / Search / Referral / Other)

**Button text:** "Let's Have a Conversation"

**Purpose:** Capture context for meaningful first call. No friction, no excessive fields. The "situation" field gates the conversion.

---

## Section 4: Alternative Contact

**H2:** "Prefer direct contact?"

"Email us at [email]. Call [phone] if you'd rather talk now."

**Purpose:** For visitors who avoid forms. Removes pressure from form path.

---

## Section 5: After Submission

**H2:** "From here"

Single paragraph:

"We'll read what you've sent, determine whether WebXC is the right fit, and respond within 24 hours. If it looks promising, we'll suggest a 30-minute call. If we think you'd be better served by someone else, we'll tell you that directly. No perpetual follow-up sequences."

**Purpose:** Final trust signal. Acknowledge that fit matters. Set response time. Promise directness.

---

## Tone Principles

- No em dashes. Use commas, full stops, or restructure.
- Active voice throughout. "We listen" not "Your input is heard."
- Short sentences (avg 12-15 words).
- Confidence without urgency. No pressure language.
- Bilingual register: business-focused, no jargon.

---

## On Successful Submission

"Thanks. We'll be in touch within 24 hours."

Optional: Link to relevant blog post or service page for reading while waiting.

---

## Key Principle

This page converts through trust, not pressure. Every element reassures the visitor that this is a genuine conversation starter, not a funnel.
