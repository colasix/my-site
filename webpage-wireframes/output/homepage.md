# Homepage Wireframe — Single Page with Contact

## Page Purpose
The homepage is the entire site. It must qualify visitors, articulate the gap between their real-world standing and digital presence, explain what WebXC does and how the engagement works, introduce James and Andy, and provide a contact mechanism. Contact section sits directly above the footer.

## Target Personas (all four, prioritised)
1. The Legacy Moderniser — established business, dated website
2. The Burned Decision Maker — paid agencies, nothing stuck
3. The Strategically Unaware Leader — traffic softening, uncertain why
4. The Ethical Medical/YMYL Specialist — reputation-first, zero risk tolerance

---

## Section 1: Hero

**Purpose:** Establish the core value proposition in one sentence. Create immediate recognition for the target audience. Two CTAs: primary (conversation) and secondary (self-assessment).

**Suggested heading:** Something that frames the gap between business quality and digital presence. Not a generic "we build websites" headline. Should make the reader think "that's me."

**Content direction:**
- Headline: frames the positioning gap as the core problem
- Sub-copy: 2-3 sentences. Principal-led. Conversion-engineered. AI-ready. Owned by you.
- Primary CTA: "Let's Have a Conversation" → scrolls to contact section
- Secondary CTA: "Take the Self-Assessment" → /workshop/

---

## Section 2: Qualification

**Purpose:** Let three persona types self-identify immediately. Each card describes a situation, not a service. The visitor should read one and think "that's exactly where I am."

**Content direction:**
- 3 cards, one per primary persona (Legacy Moderniser, Burned Decision Maker, Strategically Unaware)
- Each card: situation heading + 2-3 sentence description of what they're experiencing
- No CTA per card. The section itself acts as qualification — they read on if they recognise themselves.

---

## Section 3: The Gap

**Purpose:** Articulate the core problem WebXC solves. The gap between real-world business credibility and what the digital presence communicates. Make it concrete: generic messaging, confused journeys, AI invisibility.

**Content direction:**
- Heading: names the gap directly
- 3-4 short paragraphs building the case
- Touch on: generic content that doesn't reflect how the business wins work, AI systems indexing the wrong picture, search softening, agencies that build to briefs without questioning the commercial logic
- End with the cost: qualified enquiries and market visibility lost

---

## Section 4: How We Work

**Purpose:** Explain the commercial structure clearly. Conversation → Discovery → Option A or B. Remove ambiguity about what an engagement looks like. Build confidence in the process.

**Content direction:**
- Heading: about the process/path, not "our services"
- Step 1: Initial Conversation (free). Principal-led. Fit assessment, not sales pitch.
- Step 2: Discovery & Strategic Review (paid). Real findings, not checkbox audit. Business clarity, website assessment, AI visibility baseline, competitor comparison, prioritised recommendations.
- Step 3: Two paths. Option A: Content & Digital Authority Programme (existing site is structurally adequate, content underperforms). Option B: Complete Digital Foundation Build (platform itself is the constraint). Brief description of each.
- Emphasise: everything owned by the client

---

## Section 5: Three Outcomes

**Purpose:** Translate the service into tangible results using the three sub-page themes. What actually changes when the digital foundation is right.

**Content direction:**
- 3 cards/blocks:
  1. We increase your conversions — clear messaging, trust architecture, sales-logic layouts, 90+ PageSpeed
  2. We increase your traffic — dual-channel visibility (search + AI), semantic architecture, entity resolution
  3. We make content easier to manage — CMS independence, modular blocks, team can update without developers
- Each: outcome heading + 2-3 sentences of specifics grounded in source docs
- No fluff — concrete deliverables and measurable indicators

---

## Section 6: Principals

**Purpose:** Introduce James and Andy. Build trust through experience and directness. Reinforce that every engagement is principal-led — no handoff to juniors.

**Content direction:**
- Heading: about the people, not "our team"
- James: Strategy & Positioning. 20+ years. Business logic first. What makes you win work.
- Andy: Technical & Performance. 20+ years. 90+ Core Web Vitals. Schema markup. AI-ready platforms.
- Closing note: 40+ years combined. Started WebXC because agency work wasn't delivering what established businesses needed.

---

## Section 7: Contact

**Purpose:** Provide the primary conversion mechanism. Sits above the footer. Should feel inviting, not transactional. Reinforce: no sales pitch, principal-led conversation.

**Content direction:**
- Heading: invitation to conversation, reinforcing the tone
- Sub-copy: what happens next (respond within 24 hours, principal handles it, no pressure)
- Form fields: name, business sector (text input), email, phone (optional), preferred contact method (radio: phone/email/calendar), situation description (textarea), how they heard about us (select)
- Alternative: direct email and phone for people who prefer not to use forms
- Post-form note: what happens after submission (read, assess fit, respond within 24 hours)

---

## Internal Linking
- Hero secondary CTA → /workshop/
- No other internal page links (this is the only page)

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA text: "Let's Have a Conversation"
- Secondary CTA text: "Take the Self-Assessment"
- Contact section is the primary conversion target
- Hero CTA anchors to #contact section
