## **The Commercial Structure**

### **Entry Point: Conversation → Discovery**

Every engagement starts the same way.

**Step 1: Initial Conversation (Free)**

An exploratory, principal-led discussion to understand the business, its current digital presence, and what's prompting the enquiry. This is a fit conversation, not a sales pitch — it establishes whether WebXC's approach matches the client's situation and goals.

For clients coming from the workshop, this builds directly on the self-assessment they've already completed. The gaps they identified become the starting point.

**Step 2: Discovery & Strategic Review (Paid)**

A structured review that produces genuine strategic findings — not a templated audit. This is principal-level thinking applied to the client's specific situation. It includes:

* **Business clarity review** — how clearly the business communicates what it does, who it serves, and why it's credible — across every page and touchpoint  
* **Website assessment** — structure, content depth, semantic signals, and technical readiness  
* **AI visibility baseline** — how the business currently appears across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude (a concrete "before" picture the client can reference throughout the engagement)  
* **Competitor comparison** — how the digital presence stacks up against key competitors in both search and AI discovery  
* **Search intent mapping** — where the business is visible and invisible across the buyer journey  
* **Entity signal assessment** — how clearly the business registers as a distinct, credible entity for AI and search systems  
* **Prioritised recommendations** — what to fix, what to build, and in what order

**What the client receives:** A written report with clear findings and a recommended path forward — Option A, Option B, or specific actions the internal team can take independently.

**Why it's paid:** This work has real strategic value. It requires principal-level analysis, not checkbox auditing. Paying for it ensures mutual commitment and means the client walks away with something actionable regardless of what happens next.

**How it connects:** The discovery findings become the brief for whichever option the client chooses.

## **Sub-Page 1: We Increase Your Conversions**

*Your website should convert visitors into enquiries. If it doesn't, the problem is usually clarity, not traffic.*

### **The Problem**

Most established business websites describe what the company does — but don't make the case for why a visitor should choose them. Messaging is generic. The customer journey is confusing. Service pages are structured around the org chart, not around what buyers actually need to know. Calls-to-action are weak or buried. The site generates visits, but not the right kind of enquiries.

Upgrading to a new site built with a modern approach and clearly aligned strategy — or restructuring your current site with the same thinking — can eliminate typical pitfalls like:

* **Slow, underperforming pages** — visitors leave before the content loads, let alone converts  
* **Outdated and mobile-unfriendly layouts** — the site doesn't reflect the business's actual market standing  
* **Customer journey confusion** — visitors can't quickly find what's relevant to them, so they bounce

### **How We Solve It**

**Business Clarity & Commercial Messaging**

We clarify the business's market position, ideal customer profile, and competitive differentiation — not as a standalone "positioning project," but as the essential foundation for every piece of content we produce. This means the site says what the business actually does, who it serves best, and why it's the right choice — in language that resonates with the people making buying decisions.

A commercial messaging framework with headlines, value propositions, and proof points aligned to how the business actually wins work. Call-to-action strategy and conversion logic review to ensure the site drives qualified enquiries, not just visits.

**Conversion Pathway Design** *(Option B)*

Sales-logic page layouts structured to guide different customer types toward the right action. Enquiry forms, contact flows, and lead qualification mechanisms that balance friction with lead quality. Trust architecture — strategic placement of proof elements (accreditations, case studies, team credentials, client logos) where they influence decisions. ICP-based navigation routing different visitor types to relevant deep-dive content from the first interaction.

**Visual Design Calibrated to Market Standing** *(Option B)*

High-fidelity UI design that reflects the business's actual position in its market — established authority, not startup energy. A flexible design system with reusable content blocks so the client can build new pages and scale independently without breaking visual consistency.

### **What the Client Ends Up With**

A digital presence where every page is built to convert — clear messaging that reflects the business's genuine strengths, structured around how buyers actually evaluate options, with conversion pathways designed to generate qualified enquiries. Whether applied to an existing site (Option A) or built from scratch (Option B), the result is a site that works as hard as the business does.

## **Sub-Page 2: We Increase Your Traffic**

*Your website could be hindering your efforts in SEO and AI recognition. Do it right the first time.*

### **The Problem**

Search performance is softening across every sector. Over 65% of searches now end without a click. AI systems answer questions, compare options, and make recommendations before anyone visits a website. The businesses that maintain and grow traffic are the ones that AI and search systems can clearly understand, trust, and cite.

Most established business websites weren't built for this reality. Content was produced for volume rather than depth. Technical signals are misconfigured or missing entirely. The business's genuine expertise is implied but never evidenced in ways machines can parse. The result: declining organic traffic and near-total invisibility in AI-assisted discovery.

Website speed, structure, and technicals set the foundation for optimal SEO and AI visibility.

### **How We Solve It**

**Content Architecture & Search Intent Alignment**

A structured hierarchy of what the business offers, organised by customer need and commercial priority — not the internal org chart. Content audit and consolidation identifying thin, generic, or redundant pages for removal or merging (sometimes the highest-impact content work is subtraction). Search intent alignment across the full buyer journey so the business is visible at awareness, consideration, and decision stages.

Content development and restructuring of key pages — written to persuade the humans reading them and structured so machines can parse, verify, and cite them. FAQ and question-based content designed for AI extraction and conversational search. E-E-A-T signal implementation — surfacing credentials, case studies, evidence, and experience in formats both Google and AI systems recognise.

**Entity & Authority Signals**

Key person development — defining the people behind the business as distinct, credible entities with clear expertise signals (critical for E-E-A-T and for AI systems evaluating author authority). Business entity resolution — ensuring the business is clearly and consistently defined across its own site, structured data, directories, professional bodies, and third-party references. Entity relationship mapping — connecting services, people, credentials, locations, and proof into a coherent picture machines can interpret.

**On-Site Semantic & Structural Optimisation**

Schema markup implementation or improvement (Organisation, Person, Service, FAQ, Review, HowTo). Internal linking and page structure improvements for semantic coherence. AI crawler accessibility review and fixes (JavaScript rendering barriers, content access issues). Metadata, heading structure, and on-page semantic optimisation.

**Technical Platform Performance** *(Option B)*

Core Web Vitals optimisation targeting 90+ performance scores. Enterprise-grade security implementation. AI crawler optimisation ensuring the platform supports AI agent parsing, content extraction, and structured data retrieval. Full semantic schema markup deployed across the site from day one. LLMs.txt and AI-specific access configuration where appropriate.

**Off-Site Recommendations**

Review strategy — which platforms matter and how to generate reviews for maximum AI and search impact. Citation and directory recommendations for consistent entity presence. Earned mention opportunities — PR, industry bodies, professional associations, and content partnerships worth pursuing. Social presence recommendations aligned to the business's audience and discovery channels.

### **What the Client Ends Up With**

A digital presence calibrated for both traditional search and generative AI — content that demonstrates genuine expertise, technical signals that machines can read and trust, and entity resolution that makes the business clearly identifiable and citable. Every piece of work compounds: each page strengthens the authority of the whole.

## **Sub-Page 3: We Make It Easier for You to Manage Content**

*Complex, inefficient content management adds friction and costs to the business long term.*

### **The Problem**

Most established business websites have a content management problem that nobody talks about. The team can't update pages without a developer. Adding a new service page means waiting weeks and paying for custom work. The CMS is so tightly coupled to the design that any content change risks breaking the layout. Content sits stale because updating it is harder than it should be.

This isn't just an operational annoyance — it's a strategic liability. Businesses that can't easily publish, update, and expand their content can't respond to market changes, can't build authority through consistent publishing, and can't maintain the freshness signals that both search engines and AI systems reward.

Smart CMS design separates design from content management so your non-technical team can update content with ease.

### **How We Solve It**

**Structured, Maintainable Content Architecture** *(Both Options)*

Content structured around reusable patterns — service pages, case studies, team profiles, FAQs — so the format is consistent and the team knows exactly how to add new content without guessing. Content guidelines and style documentation so quality stays consistent as the team grows or changes. Clear separation between what the business says (content) and how it looks (design), so updating one doesn't break the other.

**Modular CMS & Content Blocks** *(Option B)*

CMS implementation on WordPress with modular content management — the client can edit, expand, and create pages without developer intervention. A flexible design system with reusable content blocks so new pages match the site's visual standards automatically. The client can build landing pages, add services, publish case studies, and expand the site independently.

**Handover & Training** *(Option B)*

Team training on CMS management, content editing, page creation, basic SEO hygiene, and structured data maintenance. The goal is full independence — the client's team should be able to manage day-to-day content operations without ongoing developer support.

### **What the Client Ends Up With**

A content management setup that works for the team that actually uses it — not just the developers who built it. Whether we're restructuring content on an existing platform (Option A) or building a new CMS from the ground up (Option B), the client ends up with a system they can maintain, expand, and evolve independently. Content stays current because keeping it current is easy.
