# WebXC Sitemap & CTA Strategy Document

## Executive Overview

This document defines the site architecture, information hierarchy, and customer call-to-action strategy for WebXC. The strategy is built around four distinct user personas and their natural entry paths into the WebXC relationship. The sitemap reflects WebXC's confidence, principal-led positioning, and the reality that established business owners respond better to peer conversation than traditional sales funnels.

---

### Silo Structure & Internal Linking Logic

WebXC operates three primary content silos, each addressing a distinct business need:

**Silo 1: Discovery & Diagnostic (Problem Identification)**
- Entry: Homepage, About, Blog
- Hub: /discovery/
- Purpose: Help business owners understand their current state and identify what needs to change
- Linking: All service pages link back to discovery as the natural "next step after understanding"

**Silo 2: Services & Solutions (Problem Solving)**
- Entry: Discovery, About, Workshop
- Hub: /services/ (optional) or direct to service pages
- Pages: Option A (Positioning & Digital Authority), Option B (Digital Foundation Build), Ongoing Programme
- Purpose: Present tailored solutions based on business readiness and challenge type
- Linking: Each service page links to the others as alternatives and to the Ongoing Programme as the natural evolution

**Silo 3: Content & Thought Leadership (Authority Building)**
- Entry: Search, social, direct
- Hub: /blog/
- Pages: Individual blog posts
- Purpose: Establish WebXC's expertise and provide free value before any conversation
- Linking: Blog posts link to relevant service pages, the workshop as entry point, and the about page for credibility

**Bridge Pages:**
- Homepage: Central hub connecting all silos
- About: Trust signal connecting to services and discovery
- Workshop: Entry funnel connecting discovery and services
- Contact: Final conversion point from all silos

### Internal Linking Principles

1. **Trust Flow**: Workshop -> Discovery -> Service Selection -> Ongoing
2. **Silo Integrity**: Pages within a silo link to each other; cross-silo links are intentional bridges
3. **Persona Routing**: Different entry paths based on persona and awareness level
4. **No Dead Ends**: Every page has a clear next step (primary CTA or contextual link)
5. **Authority Reinforcement**: Service pages link to relevant blog content; blog posts link to relevant services

## CTA Strategy & Implementation

### Primary CTAs (Site-Wide)

**Primary CTA: "Let's Have a Conversation"**
- Link: /contact/
- Placement: Header button (persistent), hero sections, page endings
- Tone: Peer-to-peer invitation, not sales funnel
- Usage: Default CTA when engagement/discovery/services are the next step
- Applies to: About, Discovery, all service pages, contact

**Secondary CTA: "Take the Self-Assessment"**
- Link: /workshop/
- Placement: Homepage, blog posts, blog hub
- Tone: Low-friction, value-first offer
- Usage: When the visitor is in research/qualification mode
- Applies to: Homepage, blog pages, may appear in service pages as alternative

**Discovery CTA: "Book Your Strategic Review"**
- Link: /discovery/
- Placement: Service pages (as pre-service step), blog posts
- Tone: Investment-focused, clarity-driven
- Usage: When the business owner is interested in services but needs diagnostic clarity first
- Applies to: Service pages, blog (depending on relevance)

**Tertiary CTA: "Learn About Our Services"**
- Link: /services/ (hub) or specific service page
- Placement: About page, blog posts, homepage
- Tone: Exploratory, no pressure
- Usage: When education and awareness are the priority
- Applies to: About, blog

### CTA Anti-Patterns & Language to Avoid

WebXC does NOT use:
- "Get a Quote" (implies transactional, not strategic)
- "Free Audit" (commoditizes the work, undermines value)
- "Request a Demo" (too agency-like)
- "Sign Up" (creates perception of mass service)
- "Download Now" (urgency language that doesn't fit peer relationship)
- "Claim Your Free" (scarcity/pressure tactics)
- "Limited Time Offer" (contradicts peer-led positioning)
- "Act Now" (aggressive, not peer-like)
- "See Pricing" (before qualification or conversation)
- Exclamation marks in CTAs (feels salesy, not confident)

WebXC DOES use:
- Conversational, direct language ("Let's Have a Conversation", "Book Your Review")
- Outcome-focused language ("Get Clear On", "Discover Your", "Build Your")
- Peer-to-peer tone (implies equals, not vendor/buyer)
- Period-ended sentences in CTA buttons (confidence, not urgency)
- Specific language (not vague, not generic)

### CTA Placement Principles

1. **Top of Page**: Hero CTA always present; must be primary CTA or journey-relevant CTA
2. **Mid-Page**: Placement after 50-60% of content (after providing value, before fatigue)
3. **End of Page**: Always include end-of-page CTA (final opportunity)
4. **Sidebar/Widget**: Optional seasonal or topic-specific secondary CTA
5. **Form Completion**: Post-submission CTA or next-step messaging
6. **No Floating CTAs**: Avoid sticky headers that feel intrusive or agency-like
7. **Single Primary Per Section**: Multiple CTAs (different destinations) confuse the user

## Internal Linking Logic & Trust Flow

### The Trust Flow Sequence

Every persona's journey follows this general trust arc, though entry points differ:

```
Awareness (Blog/Content)
    -> Interest (Homepage/About)
    -> Consideration (Workshop/Discovery)
    -> Decision (Service Selection)
    -> Commitment (Ongoing)
```

### Persona-Specific Entry Paths

**Legacy Moderniser**
1. Entry: Homepage (direct or search) or About
2. Path: About -> Services Overview -> Option B (Digital Foundation Build)
3. Optional: Discovery -> Option B
4. Conversion: "Let's Have a Conversation"
5. Outcome: Ongoing Authority & Visibility programme

**Burned Decision Maker**
1. Entry: Homepage or Workshop
2. Path: Workshop (self-assess) -> About (rebuild trust)
3. Next: Discovery -> Service Selection (Option A or B)
4. Conversion: "Let's Have a Conversation"
5. Outcome: Ongoing programme with principal oversight

**Strategically Unaware Leader**
1. Entry: Blog/Search
2. Path: Blog Post -> Blog Hub -> Homepage -> About
3. Next: Workshop (entry point) -> Discovery
4. Service: Option A (Positioning & Authority) or Option B
5. Conversion: "Let's Have a Conversation"
6. Outcome: Ongoing programme

**Medical/YMYL Specialist**
1. Entry: Homepage or Direct Search
2. Path: About (trust signals) -> Services Overview
3. Next: Option A (Positioning & Authority) for compliance-aware positioning
4. Optional: Discovery for strategic clarity
5. Conversion: "Let's Have a Conversation"
6. Outcome: Ongoing programme for sustained compliance and authority

### Silo-Specific Internal Linking

**Discovery & Diagnostic Silo**
- Pages: /discovery/, /workshop/
- Linking: Both pages link to each other (workshop as entry, discovery as next step)
- Outbound: Both link to service pages as "next steps after clarity"
- Inbound: All service pages and homepage link to discovery

**Services & Solutions Silo**
- Pages: Option A, Option B, Ongoing
- Linking: Each service page links to the other two as alternatives
- Outbound: All link to discovery as pre-service step (optional but recommended)
- Inbound: Homepage, about, blog link to services

**Content & Authority Silo**
- Pages: Blog hub, blog posts
- Linking: Hub links to all posts, posts link to each other (via "related posts")
- Outbound: Posts link to discovery, workshop, services, and about for context
- Inbound: Homepage features blog, all pages can link to relevant posts

### Cross-Silo Bridges

1. **Blog to Services**: Posts link to relevant service pages mid-post or in conclusion
2. **Services to Blog**: Service pages link to related blog content for depth
3. **About to All Silos**: About page is the trust foundation, linked from services and strategic moments
4. **Homepage as Hub**: Routes users to all silos based on their situation/persona
5. **Discovery as Bridge**: Sits between awareness and decision, connects all silos

### Linking Frequency & Density

- **Introductory Pages** (Homepage, About): 5-8 outbound links to secondary pages
- **Service Pages**: 4-6 outbound links (alternates, discovery, related blog, about)
- **Blog Hub**: Links to all posts plus featured selections
- **Blog Posts**: 3-5 contextual links (no more than 1 CTA link per 200 words)
- **Discovery**: 4-5 links to service pages and about for credibility
- **Contact Page**: Minimal outbound links, focus on form completion

### URL Linking Syntax

Use absolute URLs with trailing slashes for clarity:
- Homepage: https://webxc.co.nz/
- Internal page: https://webxc.co.nz/about/
- Service: https://webxc.co.nz/services/positioning-digital-authority/
- Blog post: https://webxc.co.nz/blog/post-slug/

## Summary

The WebXC sitemap and CTA strategy is built on four principles:

1. **Principal-Led Peer Relationship**: Every CTA, every page, every link reflects that WebXC principals are equals to the business owner, not vendors.

2. **Clear Persona Routing**: Different entry points and paths based on awareness level and challenge type (Legacy Moderniser, Burned Decision Maker, Strategically Unaware, Medical/YMYL).

3. **Trust Before Transaction**: The journey moves from content and credibility building, through self-assessment or principal conversation, to service selection, and finally to ongoing partnership.

4. **Simplicity and Directness**: The site avoids agency jargon, discount language, urgency tactics, or anything that feels salesy. The tone is confident, direct, and peer-to-peer.

The internal linking logic creates three content silos (Discovery, Services, Content) connected by a trust flow that moves visitors from awareness to engagement to decision to commitment. Every page has a clear next step, and every CTA language reflects WebXC's positioning as problem-solving peers, not vendors.
