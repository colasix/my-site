The Two Ways AI Can Know About Your Business, and Why Most Businesses Are Invisible in Both

Parametric vs non-parametric knowledge, explained for a business audience.

By Andrew Newman March 2026

When a potential customer asks ChatGPT or Perplexity to recommend a business like yours, the AI has exactly two ways to know you exist. Understanding the difference between these two pathways is the single most important thing a business owner can learn about AI visibility right now.

Pathway one: parametric knowledge (what AI has memorised)

Large language models like GPT-4 and Claude are trained on vast amounts of text data. During that training process, they absorb patterns, facts, and associations. This is called parametric knowledge. It is baked into the model's weights. It is what the AI "knows" without looking anything up.

If your business has been mentioned frequently and consistently across high-quality sources on the web, there is a chance the AI has absorbed your brand as an entity. When someone asks about your industry, the model might recall your business name, your positioning, even specific things you are known for.

But here is the problem: most NZ businesses operating in the $2M to $50M range have not generated enough digital signal to make it into an AI model's parametric memory. You might be a respected name in your industry, but if that reputation lives in handshakes, word of mouth, and trade events, the AI simply does not know you exist.

Pathway two: non-parametric knowledge (what AI can look up)

The second pathway is retrieval. When AI tools need current or specific information, they search the web in real time. This is non-parametric knowledge. Perplexity does this by default. Google's AI Overviews pull from live search results. Even ChatGPT now has web browsing capabilities.

This is where most businesses have a more immediate opportunity. If your website is well-structured, if your content clearly answers the questions your buyers are asking, and if your digital presence includes the right entity signals and structured data, AI tools can find you and cite you in their responses.

The catch is that most business websites were built for human visitors browsing via Google, not for AI systems trying to extract reliable, structured information. A beautiful brochure site with vague marketing language gives AI tools nothing to work with.

Why you need to invest in both

The short-term play is non-parametric. Fix your website's structure, add schema markup, create content that directly answers buyer questions with specificity and authority. This makes you findable when AI tools search in real time.

The long-term play is parametric. Build the kind of digital authority, through owned content, third-party mentions, and consistent entity signals, that gets absorbed into the next generation of AI training data. This is how you become a business that AI tools recommend from memory.

The businesses that act now will have a compounding advantage

AI models are retrained periodically. The content and signals that exist on the web today will influence what AI knows tomorrow. Every month you wait is a month your competitors might be building the digital footprint that gets absorbed into the next training cycle.

This is not about gaming a system. It is about making sure your real-world credibility is reflected in your digital presence, so that when AI tools are asked about what you do, they have the information they need to recommend you confidently.
